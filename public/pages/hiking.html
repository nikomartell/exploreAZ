Hiking in Arizona

Below we have listed 6 of the best hikes in Arizona, suited to a wide range of physical ability.

Grand Canyon

With guided tours and a wide range of easy, moderate and intense hikes, the Grand Canyon is a great destination for any level hiker looking to get outside. With notable trails such as the Rim Trail or Bright Angel Trail you will find yourself quickly encapsulated in one of the seven wonders of the world. The canyon offers guided tours, boat rides through the colorado river as well as restaraunts and lodging for those looking to extend their stay.

To learn more visit https://www.nps.gov/grca/planyourvisit/day-hiking.htm

7 falls hike

7 falls is a moderatley intense hike located in Sabino Canyon just next door to Tucson. With a distance of over 8 miles this hike is surely a day long activity that will not dissapoint. Hikers who find themselves making it to the end of the trail will quickly notice what it is named for as they approach the seven stacked waterfalls located at the end. On a hotter day, dont be afraid to bring a swimsuit! Because some travelers even like to swim in the resevoirs underneath the falls.

To learn more visit https://hikearizona.com/decoder.php?ZTN=120

Elden lookout hike flagstaff az

Elden trail tends to be a more intense hike then some of the previous options mentioned on this list. This is due to increases and decreases of elevation throughout the hike. Coming in at only 2.67 miles the hike may seem rather simple, but do not be misguided as the steep trails can quickly wear people out. Hikers who make it to the top will be greeted by a gorgeous view of flagstaff that you can hardly find anywhere else.

To learn more visit https://www.alltrails.com/trail/us/arizona/elden-lookout-trail

Broken arrow trail

Broken Arrow trail could be considered one of the easiest hikes on this list. Where the hiker travels through gorgeous red rock for a majority of the hike. This area is also popular for jeep tours where visitors can traverse the terrain of the redrocks inside jeeps for a more relaxed experience with all the same views. As well as jeep tours this trail tends to be popular with mountain biking communities, so if you've been meaning to get that bike out of the garage this summer, this is the spot to take it!

To learn more visit https://www.alltrails.com/trail/us/arizona/broken-arrow

Tom's thumb

A moderatley challenging trail, coming in at 4 miles, hikers will find themselves atop a beautiful mountain with a gorgeous view, as the hike increases in elevation a majority of the way as you hike it. Named for the feature hikers will see themselves heading towards, Tom's Thumb, a large rock feature jutting out of the ground. Located adjacent to Scottsdale, this hike is a great choice for anyone who finds themselves staying near the valley.

To learn more visit https://www.mcdowellsonoran.org/toms
