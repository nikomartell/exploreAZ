5 Things to do in the Arizona Summer

1. Six Flags Hurricane Harbor

Six Flags Hurricane Harbor provides a fun escape from the desert heat! The perfect retreat for children and adults alike. The park features 7 water slides and many other exciting attractions attractions.

Location: Phoenix, AZ

Cost: $45/day

Website: sixflags.com

2. Salt River Tubing

Salt River tubing is a fun time for the family! You can rent a tube out for the day and float around in the gentle river. The river is a comfortable temperature during the sumer and is sure to provide a refreshing break from the summer sun. Don't forget to bring your sunscreen!

Location: Mesa, AZ

Cost: $25 per rental tube

Website: saltrivertubing.com

3. Phoenix Hot Air Balloon Ride at Sunrise

An early morning hot air ballon ride gives you an amazing view of the desert sunrise. This one is a bit on the pricey side but it's sure to be an experience that you will never forget!

Location: Phoenix, AZ

Cost: Starts at $209

Website: viator.com

4. Visit Sedona

Sedona is known for its absolutely stunning landscape. There are many natural rock formations to see as well as a whole host of tours offered in the area. The town itself is pedestrian friendly and has lots of shops with unique souvenirs for sale. Sedona is also at a higher elevation and tends to be a bit cooler than Phoenix and Tucson.

Location: Sedona, AZ

Cost: Free

Website: visitsedona.com

5. Pima Air & Space Museum

The Pima Air & Space Museum features over 400 historic aircraft ranging from the early to modern. The museum includes both military and civilian aircraft and is sure to be exciting for any aircraft enthusiast. Aircraft are displayed in hangar and on the museum grounds so you can stay in the air conditioning if it gets too hot outside.

Location: Tucson, AZ
