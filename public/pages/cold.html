Cold Weather Activities

Believe it or not, even in Arizona we tend to get a cold winter.

Here are six activities you and your family can try do when looking to get out of the house on a cold winters day.

Snow Bowl

Ever wanted to try your hand at skiing or snowboarding, but never found the right time? Arizona's Snowbowl is the perfect location for first timers and even advanced riders to enjoy a colder day in the Arizona winter. With lodging for extended stays, delicious local restaraunts and incredibly scenic views snowbowl is the perfect place to spend some time in the snow without breaking the bank.

To learn more visit https://www.snowbowl.ski/

Downtown Flagstaff

For those less inclined to a day in the snow, we would suggest taking a day to explore downtown Flagstaff. Flagstaff has a diverse population and is centered right in the woods, so the views never get old. You can find a wide range of local shops, restaraunts and bars for all patrons to enjoy. Just outside of Flagstaff is a number of hikes and activities for those to enjoy, even local spots to go sledding for those interested in time in the snow. Flagstaff is also centered around a variety of hikes, some of which this website mentions like Elden Trail, its certain to say Flagstaff has plenty of activities for a family looking to enjoy their winter.

To learn more visit https://www.flagstaffarizona.org/

Zoo Lights

Ever wondered where the zoo animals go during a cold winter? Well at Phoenix Zoo, they go christmas shopping before they decorate the park! Jokes aside, Zoo lights is a gorgeous family friendly experience that can be done on a budget. The Zoo showcases a wide range of christmas lights, while staying on theme with the furry friends they take care of. If you decide to go to Zoo Lights dont forget to bring cocoa and some warm clothes! It certainly is a memorable experience.

To learn more visit https://www.phoenixzoo.org/events/zoolights/

Sabino Canyon Tours

Located in southern Arizona Sabino Canyon is a gorgeous slice of the desert right outside of Tucson. A great time to visit it is the winter because of the cooler weather, making it an activity that wont feel so exhausting. With tours hosted year round Sabino Canyon is a great way to experience the ecosystem of the Arizona desert without breaking the bank or finding yourself sunburned and tired.

To learn more visit https://sabinocanyoncrawler.com/sabino-canyon-tours/

Mount Lemmon

Mount Lemmon is another location just outside of Tucson on this list. Located over 9,000 feet above Tucson the views will surely not dissapoint. Atop the mountain is a local community full of friendly people, restaraunts and wonderful views. It really feels like an immersive getaway into the woods, just one and a half hours outside of Tucson. First timers will want to try the cookie cabin where they offer a range of cookies that will not dissapoint!

To learn more visit https://www.visittucson.org/things-to-do/outdoors/mountains/mount-lemmon/

Desert Botanical Gardens

Similar to Phoenix Lights, Desert Botanical Gardens finds themselves outfitting the gardens with holiday cheer as the colder months approach. During the daytime, visitors can find breathtaking displays of Arizona's natural ecosystem and wildlife, while at night, something a little brighter takes the stage. Through the gardens you can experience gorgeous light displays that will leave everyone in awe and during the daytime you can explore the display of Arizona's natural flora while learning more about our State.

To learn more visit https://dbg.org/
