Girls Weekend in Sedona!

Jessie Smith

Age range: 18-25 , Trip type: Group

I spent this past weekend with my friends in Sedona and it was amazing! We kicked off our adventure with a sunrise hike at Fay Canyon – the perfect way to start the day with a dose of nature's beauty. And let me tell you, those views of Courthouse Butte and Bell Rock were absolutely breathtaking!

After working up an appetite, we headed to the Coffee Pot Restaurant for breakfast. But the fun didn't stop there! We tackled Sugarloaf Loop and even ventured into Keyhole Cave for a little extra excitement. And what better way to end the day than with a mouthwatering dinner at Pisa Lisa? That pizza was worth every bite, let me tell you!

We started bright and early on day 2 with a sunrise hike at Cathedral Rock. The trail was busy, but we woke up early and for those views? Absolutely worth it! After our hike, we headed to The Secret Garden Cafe at Tlaquepaque Arts & Shopping Village for some breakfast. Their outdoor seating area was the perfect spot to soak up the morning sun and fuel up for the day ahead. With our bellies full, we spent the afternoon exploring the charming streets of Tlaquepaque, indulging in a little retail therapy and soaking in the Spanish-inspired architecture. It was like stepping into another world!

We had so much fun and I can’t wait to visit Sedona again!

Father's Journey with Kids: Exploring Grand Canyon!

David Anderson

Age range: 26-35, Trip type: Family

Hey there fellow adventurers!

David here, sharing our unforgettable journey through Arizona with my two little explorers, Emily and Ethan.

We set out on our road trip armed with nothing but excitement and a thirst for adventure. Our first stop? The magnificent Grand Canyon! We stumbled upon this gem while browsing through recommendations on our favorite travel website, and boy, were we blown away!

Standing on the rim of the canyon, Emily, Ethan, and I were speechless. The sheer grandeur of the canyon stretched out before us, a testament to the incredible beauty of nature.

We followed the winding trails, discovering hidden caves and ancient rock formations along the way. With each step, we felt more connected to the land and each other.

As the sun dipped below the horizon, painting the sky in hues of orange and pink, we knew that our adventure had only just begun. Thanks to the helpful tips and recommendations from our fellow travelers on the website, we were able to create memories that will last a lifetime.

Until next time, happy trails!
